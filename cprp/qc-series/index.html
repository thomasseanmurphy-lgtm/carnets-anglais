📖 The story

Read me first!

Théo runs the final quality control of a series of 200 steel shafts. First, he takes a sample from the batch. Each shaft is measured with a caliper and a micrometer, and every dimension is compared to the nominal value on the drawing. A part that is out of tolerance is a non-conforming part, and it is put aside.

So far, Théo has checked fifty parts. Most of them are within the limits, but two shafts are 0.03 mm larger than the maximum size — they are too big. He found the same deviation yesterday, so the reject rate is higher than last week.

The team leader said that the whole batch had to be inspected 100 %. Théo thinks the tool must be worn, because the parts can't all drift by chance. He has written a non-conformity report, and the series has been put on hold until the machine is checked. ✅
