📖 The story

Read me first!

Hugo is a machinist in a metalworking workshop. He operates a CNC lathe and a milling machine. Every day, the cutting tools produce a lot of swarf, and the machines use coolant to stay cool. Safety comes first: operators must wear glasses, and they mustn't pour used coolant into the drain.

Last month, the workshop started a big recycling drive. First, the team sorted the waste into three skips: one for scrap metal, one for plastic and one for cardboard. Then Hugo emptied the coolant tank and sent the old fluid to a treatment plant. The drive was harder than expected, but the results were better than last year.

Since the drive began, the team has collected two tonnes of scrap metal and has recycled almost 90% of its waste. They haven't sent any swarf to landfill yet. Hugo says recycling is cheaper than throwing metal away, and it is one of the best things the workshop has ever done. 🌱

Rules & advice (modals)

8 pts
  1. You wear safety glasses near the machines.
  2. You pour used coolant into the drain — it's illegal.
  3. Every operator sort the swarf before the break.
  4. The bins are labelled, so you guess where the waste goes.
  5. If a skip is full, you call the supplier.
  6. You leave oily rags near the machines; it's a fire risk.
  7. Visitors enter the workshop without a helmet.
  8. We cut our waste if we want the ISO 14001 label.
