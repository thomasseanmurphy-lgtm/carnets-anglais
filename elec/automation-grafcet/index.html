📖 The story

Read me first!

Lucas is an automation technician. This week, he designs a new GRAFCET for an automated drilling station. On the line, a conveyor brings the metal parts one by one. A proximity sensor detects each part, and a pneumatic cylinder clamps it before the drill turns. Every movement is controlled by a PLC.

First, the operator selects the automatic mode. As soon as a part is detected, step 1 becomes active and the part is clamped. Subsequently, the drill goes down and makes the hole. When the limit switch is triggered, the cylinder retracts; therefore the cycle can start again. If a sensor sends no signal, the transition is not validated and the machine waits.

Safety comes first. The technician must switch off the power before he opens the cabinet, and operators mustn't bypass a safety sensor. If the operator presses the emergency stop, all the outputs will be deactivated immediately. Since Monday, Lucas has tested every step, and the station has produced 300 parts without a single fault. ⚡

Safety rules (modals)

8 pts

Obligation, interdiction ou absence d'obligation ?

  1. You open the electrical cabinet while the PLC is powered on.
  2. Before any work on the machine, the technician switch off the main power.
  3. Every new operator follow the lock-out procedure.
  4. The program is already loaded, so you write it again.
  5. If the cycle looks abnormal, you check the GRAFCET step by step.
  6. You wear insulating gloves when you work inside the cabinet.
  7. Operators bypass a safety sensor to save time.
  8. To get the CE marking, the machine have a working emergency stop.
