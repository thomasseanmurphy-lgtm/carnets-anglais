I. Reading: The Day the Transformer Failed

A transformer is a key part of any high-voltage installation. It changes the voltage so that factories can use electricity safely. When a transformer works well, nobody notices it. But when it fails, the whole plant can stop in a second.

This is exactly what happened last March at a food-processing plant. The site uses a large oil-filled transformer to feed its heavy machines. One morning, the maintenance team heard a loud bang. Smoke came out of the transformer, and oil started to leak from the bottom of the tank. All the machines downstream lost their power immediately.

The chief technician, Marco, has worked at the plant for fifteen years, so the manager called him first. "We need you here now," she told him. Marco arrived in ten minutes. He looked at the transformer and said to his team: "Don't touch it yet. Safety first."

The technicians took an oil sample to find the cause. The laboratory ran a Dissolved Gas Analysis for them. The results were clear: the oil contained too much moisture, and water had destroyed the insulation. An internal electric arc had then burned the windings.

The team could not repair the unit quickly, so they decided to bring a mobile transformer to the site. It arrived three days later. They connected it carefully and gave the plant its power back.

Since that day, the company has tested the oil every month. Good maintenance protects the equipment — and it protects the workers too.

III. Grammar: Verb Tenses

Present Simple vs Past Simple vs Present Perfect.

Reminder — the three tenses

TenseUseTypical time markers
Present Simplefacts & habits (things that are always or generally true) every day, usually, often, always, normally, once a month
Past Simplea finished action at a finished past time yesterday, last March, in 2023, two days ago, when I was an apprentice
Present Perfecta link with now / an unfinished period of time since, for, already, yet, ever, never, just, recently, so far

Quick test:

  • Is it always true / a routine? → Present Simple (The transformer changes the voltage.)
  • Is it over, at a clear past moment? → Past Simple (The transformer failed last March.)
  • Does it connect the past to now (no exact date)? → Present Perfect (The company has tested the oil since that day.)

IV. Grammar: To vs For & Object Pronouns

Reminder — TO vs FOR

TO

  • + verb = purpose / goal: He came to repair the transformer.
  • direction / recipient: She said to him, "We need you here now."

FOR

  • + noun / -ing = beneficiary: The lab ran the test for the team.
  • duration: Marco has worked here for fifteen years.
  • reason / use: It is a tool for testing the oil.
